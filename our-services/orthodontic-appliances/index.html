Why Early Orthodontic Evaluation Matters?

Orthodontists recommend that children have their first orthodontic evaluation by age 7 — even if no visible issues are present. At this age, the jaw and teeth are still developing, making it easier to detect problems early.

  • Spot Hidden Issues Early
  • Create Room for Incoming Teeth
  • Prevent Damage to Teeth or Gums
  • Monitor Growth at the Right Stages
  • Boost Confidence During Development Years

Clear Aligners

Clear aligners are custom-made, transparent trays that gently move your teeth into alignment. They're removable, comfortable, and nearly invisible — making them a popular choice for teens and adults seeking a discreet treatment option. Worn for 20–22 hours a day and replaced every 1–2 weeks.

Retainers

Retainers are custom-made appliances worn after orthodontic treatment to maintain the new position of your teeth. After active treatment ends, your teeth can shift back to their original places if not held in position. Wearing your retainer as directed is a vital final step to protect your investment in orthodontic care.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does orthodontic treatment take?

Treatment typically takes 12-24 months depending on the complexity of your case. Minor alignment issues can be corrected in as little as 6 months.

Are clear aligners as effective as braces?

For most cases, clear aligners are equally effective. However, complex cases involving significant bite issues may still require traditional braces.

How often do I need to wear my retainer?

After active treatment, wear your retainer full-time for the first 6 months, then at night indefinitely to maintain results.

Is orthodontic treatment only for children?

No, orthodontic treatment is effective at any age. Many adults successfully straighten their teeth with clear aligners or braces.
